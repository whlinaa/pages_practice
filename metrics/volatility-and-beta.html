Volatility

Volatility measures how widely returns fluctuate around their average return.

Simple calculation flow:

  • Collect periodic returns (for example monthly).
  • Compute the average return.
  • Compute the standard deviation of returns.

Volatility Example

Monthly returns: 2%, -1%, 3%, 0%, 1%, -2%

  • Average monthly return ≈ 0.5%
  • Monthly volatility (std. dev.) ≈ 1.87%
  • Annualized volatility ≈ 1.87% × √12 ≈ 6.48%

Higher volatility means larger expected swings in returns.

Beta

Beta measures sensitivity to market moves.

Formula: Beta = Covariance(Stock, Market) / Variance(Market)

  • Beta ≈ 1: moves with the market
  • Beta > 1: more sensitive than the market
  • Beta < 1: less sensitive than the market

Beta Example

Suppose:

  • Covariance(stock, market) = 0.0036
  • Variance(market) = 0.0024

Step: Beta = 0.0036 / 0.0024 = 1.5

Interpretation: if the market moves +1%, the stock is expected to move about +1.5% on average (and similarly on downside moves).
